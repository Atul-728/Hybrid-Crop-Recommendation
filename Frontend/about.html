Our Story

Who We Are.
Why We Do It.

CropOracle was born from a simple belief — that every farmer deserves access to the same intelligent tools as large-scale agribusinesses.

🎯

Our Mission

India's agricultural sector feeds over a billion people — yet farming decisions are still largely driven by tradition, intuition, and incomplete information. The result? Wrong crops in wrong soil, poor yields, wasted investment, and financial uncertainty that affects millions of families every year.

"We don't just tell you what to grow. We tell you how confident the AI is — and what that means for your specific land."

— CropOracle founding principle

CropOracle exists to bridge this gap with hybrid machine learning — combining five expert models in a stacked ensemble, powered by NGBoost's probabilistic predictions. It's not about replacing farmer wisdom; it's about amplifying it with cold, honest data.

👥

The Team Behind CropOracle

CropOracle was developed as a capstone research project by a computer science engineering team passionate about applying responsible AI to real-world agricultural challenges in India.

Our interdisciplinary team combined expertise in machine learning, full-stack development, data engineering, and agricultural science to build a system that is technically rigorous and practically useful for Indian farmers.

The project is open-source, academically grounded, and committed to transparency in AI decision-making — every prediction comes with SHAP-based explanation, not just a black-box answer.

🏆

History & Milestones

  • Phase 1

    Research & Dataset Curation

    Collected and analyzed agricultural datasets spanning 22+ crops across all Indian agro-climatic zones. Established baseline models.

  • Phase 2

    Hybrid Model Architecture

    Designed stacked ensemble combining XGBoost, CatBoost, LightGBM, Random Forest, and NGBoost as meta-learner for uncertainty quantification.

  • Phase 3

    Full-Stack Application Development

    Built FastAPI backend, Jinja-powered frontend, PostgreSQL database with full authentication, OTP verification, and Google OAuth.

  • Phase 4

    GenAI Integration & Deployment

    Integrated Gemini AI for smart crop suggestions and chatbot support. Deployed live on Railway with Docker containerization.

  • Phase 5

    DevOps & Production Hardening

    Added Kubernetes orchestration, Jenkins CI/CD pipeline, Prometheus monitoring, and Grafana dashboards for infrastructure observability.

Technology Stack

CropOracle is built on a production-grade, modern technology stack chosen for performance, reliability, and academic rigor.

🐍 FastAPI
High-performance Python web framework for the backend API
🤖 NGBoost
Probabilistic boosting for uncertainty-aware predictions
⚡ XGBoost
Gradient boosted trees — base learner in the ensemble
🐱 CatBoost
Handles categorical features like region without encoding
💡 LightGBM
Leaf-wise boosting for fast, accurate predictions
🧩 SHAP
SHapley Additive exPlanations for model interpretability
🐘 PostgreSQL
Relational database for users, logs, and predictions
✨ Gemini AI
Google's GenAI for smart crop suggestions & chatbot
🐋 Docker + K8s
Containerized deployment with Kubernetes orchestration

How to Use CropOracle

Getting your first AI crop recommendation takes less than 2 minutes:

1

Create a Free Account

Sign up with your email or continue with Google. All accounts require OTP email verification for security.

2

Navigate to "Predict"

After login, click "Predict Crop" in the navigation bar to open the prediction form.

3

Enter Soil & Climate Parameters

Input Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium levels, soil pH, temperature, humidity, and rainfall. Select your Region → State → City. The AI will auto-suggest recommended values based on your location.

4

Click "Predict Best Crop"

Our 5-model ensemble analyzes your data and returns top crop recommendations ranked by confidence, profit, and SHAP-driven explanations.

5

Review History & Analytics

All predictions are saved to "My Logs". Use the Analytics page to spot seasonal patterns and track your prediction history over time.
